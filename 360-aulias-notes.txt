🟨 CARD 2: Hazrat Shah Paran (R.A.)
Relation: Nephew of Shah Jalal
Title: Qutub of Sylhet
Legacy:

Born in Hadhramaut, he traveled with his uncle to Sylhet. After Shah Jalal’s mission, Shah Paran (R.A.) continued to guide the people of the region. He is remembered for building mosques, water tanks, and teaching centers. His shrine on a hilltop in Khadim Nagar, Sylhet is a major pilgrimage destination.
🟨 CARD 3: Hazrat Shah Jad Ali al-Yemeni (R.A.)
Origin: Yemen
Role: Early Missionary & Commander
Legacy:

A key lieutenant in the military and spiritual campaign of Shah Jalal (R.A.), Shah Jad Ali helped in organizing new Muslim communities post-victory. He was respected for his bravery and guidance in converting the people of rural Sylhet to Islam through gentle preaching and character.
🟨 CARD 4: Hazrat Shah Ali al-Yemeni (R.A.)
Origin: Yemen
Mission: Community Builder & Scholar
Legacy:

An important educator among the Auliyas, he helped set up learning centers and khanqahs (Sufi lodges) in newly Islamized villages. He is known for translating core Islamic values into local culture, helping communities blend faith and identity peacefully.
🟨 CARD 5: Hazrat Shah Arif al-Yemeni (R.A.)
Origin: Yemen
Title: Arif Billah (“The One Who Knows Allah”)
Legacy:

Known for his intense spirituality, Shah Arif was among the most reflective of the companions. He lived a life of asceticism, trained disciples in dhikr (remembrance), and emphasized inner purification. He helped spread Sufi ethics across Sylhet’s hill regions.
🟨 CARD 6: Hazrat Shah Kamal al-Yemeni (R.A.)
Origin: Yemen / Possibly Mecca
Likely Identity: Shah Kamal Quhafah of Shaharpara
Legacy:

He is credited with founding the spiritual village of Shaharpara in Sunamganj. A Qurayshi by descent, he set up institutions for religious education, spiritual retreat, and community governance. His lineage gave rise to one of Sylhet’s most influential religious families.
🟨 CARD 7: Hazrat Shah Muhammad Ayub al-Yemeni (R.A.)
Origin: Yemen
Role: Da’ee (Missionary Preacher)
Legacy:

After the conquest, he settled in a remote village, converting locals through kindness and example. He emphasized family, moral conduct, and agriculture as means to live a God-conscious life. Oral histories speak of his healing abilities and sincerity.
🟨 CARD 8: Hazrat Shah Tajuddin al-Qureshi (R.A.)
Origin: Quraysh tribe (Arabian Peninsula)
Role: Jurist and Scholar
Legacy:

He brought Qurayshi jurisprudential knowledge to Sylhet. Known for his scholarship and calm temperament, Shah Tajuddin acted as a qadi (Islamic judge) in early Muslim settlements. His fatwas were considered authoritative and paved the way for formal Sharia-based local governance.
🟨 CARD 9: Hazrat Shah Halim Uddin al-Qureshi (R.A.)
Lineage: Descendant of Quraysh
Mission: Social Reformer
Legacy:

Focused on the rights of the poor and just distribution of zakat. Shah Halim Uddin helped organize early systems for welfare in the Sylhet countryside. His teachings urged Muslims to integrate service to humanity with devotion to Allah.
